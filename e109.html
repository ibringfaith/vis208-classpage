    Tips For Doing Well on Exams
    1. Don't cram, especially the night before the exam.
    2. Manage your time wisely.
    3. Proceed from Easy to Hard.
    4. Annotate/Revise/Make-Your-Own Study Guide.
    5. Use practice exams.
    6. Rewrite your notes.
    7. Do your homework.
    8. Don't Stress.

    How I Study
    1. Read your assigned readings before the lecture.
    2. In Class: Write notes and ask questions.
    3. After Class: Make a study guide and study it.
    4. The weekend after class review your study guide
    5. Put your test days on your calendar. Decide when and what you are studying each week.
    Study Tip #8
    Don't study without breaks.
    Make sure to relax your mind.
    It can be every 25 minutes
    or every 3 hours!
    When you need a push:
    Write revision notes, do your readings, write on those readings, make flash cards, read every wider reading novel (analyse these novels), read your required readings (analyse these too), go over flash cards every morning/afternoon, buy coffee/hot chocolate in the mornings and put in a thermos, keep a necessities pouch in your bag, use a planner, wash your hair, go to commitments, attend school events, attend events you are invited too, watch movies, be kind to yourself, listen to music, SLEEP, and most importantly be proud of yourself.
    Live, Love & Learn Study Tips
    • Schedule Study Time: If something urgent comes up, reschedule your study time.
    • Use Subject Blocking: Instead of studying one subject for 3 hours straight, do 3 different subjects for 1 hour each.
    • Switch It Up: Use different studying techniques to keep yourself interested.
    • Get Enough Sleep
    • Silence Distractions: Turn on non-distracting instrumental music if you need background noise.
    Motivation and Study Tips
    • Treats: Some (healthy) snacks and your favorite tea and/or coffee.
    • Organize your desk area.
    • Supplies: Nice pens and penciils can make studying way more fun. Use sticky notes, flags, and a bullet journal to help you stay organized.
    • Drink lots of water.
    • Decorate your planner, journal, notebooks, paper, pencilcase, desk, anything you want. It's more fun to study with pretty things. Use shapes, diagrams, and arrows.
    Be positive!
    Think positive and encourage yourself. Your mindset is more important than you think, so keep motivating yourself and telling yourself that you are going to ace your exams.
